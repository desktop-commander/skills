# Prompt Templates — Job Search Advisor

## Step 1 Opening (short, warm)

> Drop your CV file and any public links — LinkedIn, GitHub, blog, portfolio — and I'll take it from there.
> The less you have to type, the better. I'll read what I can and ask only what I can't find.

---

## Step 2 Socratic Interview Starter (share with user if they want to run this themselves)

```
Here is my background: [paste CV or short description].

Act as a job search consultant. Research me online if you can.
Tell me who I am professionally, then interview me — one question at a time —
to figure out what directions I should actually be exploring.
What do you need from me to get started?
```

---

## Step 3 Job Search Prompt

```
Based on my profile, search for roles that genuinely fit me.
My constraints: [remote/hybrid], [region/timezone], [full-time/part-time], [salary target if known].

Search across multiple providers. Also scrape careers pages of any promising companies directly.
For each match, explain WHY it fits my specific background — not just keyword overlap.
Estimate salary even if not listed. Be honest about weak-fit areas.
Give me the top 5.
```

---

## Step 4 Tailored CV Prompt

```
I want to apply for [Role] at [Company].
Job posting: [URL or paste].

Write a narrow CV targeted only at this role.
- Professional summary written for this specific job
- "Why I fit [Company]" section with concrete evidence from my background
- Experience filtered to what's relevant — cut the rest
- Public work (blog/YouTube/open source) only if it strengthens the case
- Do not add skills I'd struggle to prove in an interview

Save as HTML and open in browser.
```

---

## Step 5 Interview Prep Prompt

```
I'm preparing to interview for [Role] at [Company].
Job posting: [URL].

1. What are the 5-8 most likely interview questions for this role?
2. Where are my 2-3 weakest areas for this specific job — and what are honest, credible ways to address them?
3. What are 2-3 smart questions I should ask the interviewer?
```
