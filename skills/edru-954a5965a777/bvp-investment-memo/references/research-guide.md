# Research Guide: Finding Company Data via Web Search

When writing an investment memo with incomplete inputs, use this guide to research the company before asking the user for anything.

---

## What to Always Research (Never Ask If Searchable)

### Company basics
- Search: `[company name]` — get the homepage, one-liner, product description
- Search: `[company name] crunchbase` — funding history, total raised, investors, founding date
- Search: `[company name] linkedin` — founder backgrounds, team size, location, org structure
- Search: `[company name] techcrunch` OR `[company name] funding` — recent rounds, press coverage

### Founder backgrounds
- Search each founder name on LinkedIn
- Look for: prior companies, domain expertise, exits, engineering vs. business profile
- Check: university, previous employer (especially if relevant — e.g., ex-Amazon like PagerDuty's founder)

### Product
- Visit the company website directly — read the homepage, pricing page, "how it works"
- Search: `[company name] pricing` — extract pricing tiers
- Search: `[company name] app store` OR `[company name] github` — product validation
- Search: `[company name] review` OR `[company name] G2` OR `[company name] ProductHunt` — user feedback signals

### Market size
- Search: `[industry] market size TAM [year]`
- Search: `[industry] Gartner forecast` OR `[industry] Forrester report`
- Use comparable companies as proxies: "If Twilio is worth $X, the API telephony market is at least $Y"

### Competition
- Search: `[company name] competitors` OR `[company name] vs [known competitor]`
- Search: `[industry] startups` OR `[industry] landscape` — find the competitive map
- Check G2, Capterra, or similar category pages

### Traction signals (public)
- Search: `[company name] revenue` OR `[company name] ARR` — sometimes disclosed in press
- Search: `[company name] customers` OR `[company name] enterprise` — customer announcements
- Check app store ratings and review counts as a proxy for user volume
- Check SimilarWeb estimates for consumer companies (web traffic proxy)
- Check LinkedIn employee count growth as a hiring/traction proxy

---

## What Cannot Be Found Publicly (Always Ask User)

These are almost never public and must come from the user or the company:

- **Internal MRR/ARR** (unless disclosed in a press interview)
- **Churn rate**
- **CAC and CAC payback**
- **Gross margin %**
- **Monthly burn rate**
- **Current runway**
- **Deal terms** (check size, pre-money valuation, ownership %) at early stage
- **Pipeline and bookings data**
- **Cohort retention curves**
- **Customer diligence notes** (what did reference calls reveal?)
- **Why BVP specifically** (fund fit, relationship, sourcing story)

---

## How to Ask the User (Consolidated Single Ask)

After web research, ask everything in one message. Format:

---
To complete the memo, I need a few things I couldn't find publicly:

**Required for financials:**
1. Current MRR or ARR, and YoY or MoM growth rate
2. Gross margin %
3. Monthly burn rate and cash on hand

**Required for deal section:**
4. Check size BVP is considering, pre-money valuation, target ownership %

**Would significantly improve the memo:**
5. Pitch deck (PDF) — if you have one, please share it
6. Any churn, CAC, or LTV data the company has shared
7. Key notes from customer reference calls (if diligence has started)
8. Why BVP / sourcing context (how we found them, relationship history)

If any of these are unknown or TBD, just say so and I'll mark them as [TBD] in the memo.

---

## Pitch Deck Processing

If the user shares a pitch deck (PDF), extract:
- Company one-liner (usually slide 1-2)
- Problem / solution framing
- Product description and screenshots
- Market size slide (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Traction / metrics slide — extract all numbers
- Team slide — names, titles, prior companies
- Competition / landscape slide
- Ask / terms slide (fundraising amount, use of proceeds)

Cross-reference deck claims against web research. Note any discrepancies.

---

## Research Quality Signals

**High confidence** (cite directly):
- Company press release or official blog post
- Crunchbase funding data
- LinkedIn team page (team size)
- Pricing page (pricing tiers)

**Medium confidence** (use with caveat):
- Third-party press coverage (may be outdated)
- App store ratings (proxy, not actual revenue)
- SimilarWeb traffic estimates (rough proxy)
- G2/Capterra reviews (sentiment proxy)

**Low confidence** (use only as directional):
- Forum posts or community discussions
- Social media mentions
- Unofficial "list" articles

Always note source and date when citing research in the memo.
