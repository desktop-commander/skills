# Stage Templates: Memo Structure by Round

Drawn from 19 real BVP memos grouped by stage.

---

## Seed Stage Template
*Examples: Dropcam ($200K seed), Wix ($2M seed), Twilio ($125K seed extension)*

**Length:** 3-5 pages  
**Financial data:** Minimal — MRR, customer count, capital raised so far  
**Tone:** More speculative, more personal judgment

### Section Order:
1. **Opening recommendation** — investment amount, round, valuation, why now
2. **Product** — disproportionately large section; explain every feature and the insight behind it
3. **Market** — size the opportunity; note if unproven
4. **Team** — often 2nd or 3rd section; the founder bet is often the whole thesis
5. **Competition** — brief; identify who they'll face at Series A
6. **Go-to-Market** — early signals only; organic traction if any
7. **Traction** — very limited; focus on growth rate and capital efficiency
8. **Deal** — terms, co-investors, structure
9. **Risks / Additional Questions** — longer than usual; name what BVP still needs to verify
10. **Conclusion** — often 2-3 sentences

**Key seed memo patterns:**
- "We recommend watching the demo video" — product experience stands in for hard data
- Capital efficiency is highlighted: "raised $850K, generating $10K MRR" (Auth0), "$1M raised, profitable" (Shopify)
- Risks section often doubles as a due diligence checklist: "questions to answer before Series A"
- Scenario analysis usually absent; instead: "We are positioning for the Series A lead"

## Series A Template
*Examples: Shopify, Pinterest, Auth0, Fiverr, Yelp, Rocket Lab, LinkedIn, LifeLock*

**Length:** 5-8 pages  
**Financial data:** Full traction section with key metrics  
**Tone:** Thesis-driven; this is where the investment conviction is most explicitly argued

### Section Order:
1. **Opening recommendation** — full investment ask + urgency framing if applicable
2. **Market Opportunity** — sized TAM with named macro tailwinds; often 2-3 named themes
3. **Product** — deep product explanation with pricing, ecosystem, differentiator
4. **Traction / Financials** — MRR, customer count, growth rate, basic unit economics
5. **Customer Acquisition** — organic vs. paid, CAC, virality
6. **Competition** — name specific competitors and structural reasons they can't win
7. **Team** — CEO character read + named gaps
8. **Deal** — full terms with implied revenue multiple
9. **Risks** — 4-7 genuine risks
10. **Scenario Analysis** — 3-scenario outcomes table
11. **Conclusion**
12. **Appendix** (optional) — deeper metrics, customer lists

**Key Series A patterns:**
- Opening paragraph often includes retrospective context: "We have been tracking this company for X months"
- Market section often includes a "BVP roadmap" paragraph connecting to portfolio thesis
- The "organic growth" signal gets its own paragraph if present
- Team section names specific missing VP (e.g., "no VP of Marketing", "no CFO")
- Scenario analysis uses simple revenue multiples, not DCF

## Series B+ Template
*Examples: PagerDuty ($180M pre), SendGrid ($60M pre), Mindbody ($42M pre), Toast, ServiceTitan, Twitch*

**Length:** 8-12 pages  
**Financial data:** Full SaaS metrics pack; P&L table; cohort data  
**Tone:** Data-heavy; the business is proven, now the question is scale

### Section Order:
1. **Opening recommendation** — investment ask; "following X's presentation on [date]"
2. **Original Summary** (if updating a prior memo) — recap of earlier thesis + what changed
3. **Executive summary / Why we're excited** — 4-6 bullet points with key metrics
4. **Topline Metrics** — detailed metrics table; often with historical charts described
5. **Product** — current state + expansion thesis ("big bet")
6. **Market** — updated TAM; expansion into adjacent segments
7. **Go-to-Market / Sales Execution** — detailed: pipeline, rep productivity, quotas, funnel
8. **Customer Diligence** — direct quotes from reference calls
9. **Competition** — named competitors with market share context
10. **Financials** — P&L summary; budget vs. actual; cash position
11. **Team** — executive team assessment; CFO, VP Sales, VP Marketing named
12. **Deal** — full terms; often includes secondary / founder liquidity
13. **Risks** — updated risks; may include "what would make us wrong"
14. **Outcomes Analysis** — 3-scenario table with more precise assumptions
15. **Conclusion**
16. **Appendix** — detailed cohort tables, customer lists, P&L detail

**Key Series B+ patterns:**
- Opening paragraph often quotes the exact ARR multiple: "14x June ARR" (PagerDuty)
- SaaS benchmark language: "metrics are in the right column of our framework" (PagerDuty)
- Net negative churn highlighted as a "best-in-class" signal when present
- "Platform expansion" thesis is the growth story: what the company becomes, not what it is
- Acquirer list provided as downside floor: specific strategic buyers named
- Sales funnel metrics get their own section (trials, conversions, quotas, rep ramp)

## Key Differences Summary

| Element | Seed | Series A | Series B+ |
|---------|------|----------|-----------|
| Length | 3-5 pages | 5-8 pages | 8-12 pages |
| Financial depth | Minimal | Medium | Full SaaS pack |
| Scenario analysis | Rare | Standard | Detailed |
| Sales metrics | None | Basic | Full funnel |
| Customer diligence | Rarely | Sometimes | Always |
| Retrospective | N/A | N/A | Sometimes |
| Acquirer floor | Never | Rarely | Usually |
| Founder bet weight | Very high | High | Medium |
| Market sizing | Rough | Detailed | Bottoms-up |
| Organic growth emphasis | Very high | High | Medium |
